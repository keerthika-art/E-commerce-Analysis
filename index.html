E-commerce Customer Behavior Analysis

Interactive dashboard showcasing customer segmentation and spending patterns

Project Overview

This analysis explores customer behavior patterns in an e-commerce platform using a comprehensive dataset of customer interactions and transactions. The dashboard provides insights into customer spending habits, segmentation, satisfaction levels, and demographic patterns to help businesses make data-driven decisions.

Key Demographic Insights
  • San Francisco customers spend the most on average ($1,459.77), followed by New York ($1,165.04)
  • Male customers spend more on average ($986.93) compared to female customers ($703.83)
  • There's a negative correlation between age and spending (-0.68), suggesting younger customers tend to spend more

Spending Patterns

Spending by Gender

Male customers show higher average spending compared to female customers.

Spending by Membership Type

Gold members spend significantly more than Silver and Bronze members.

Age vs Spending

Negative correlation between age and spending, with younger customers spending more.

Spending by Discount Status

Customers without discounts actually spend more on average than those with discounts.

Key Spending Insights
  • Gold members spend 75% more than Silver members and 177% more than Bronze members
  • Customers without discounts spend 15% more on average than those with discounts
  • Satisfied customers spend more than twice as much as neutral or unsatisfied customers

Business Recommendations
Targeted Marketing

Develop personalized strategies for different customer segments: reward Champions, re-engage At Risk customers, and convert Loyal Customers to Champions.

Membership Program

Optimize the membership program to encourage upgrades from Bronze to Silver and Silver to Gold tiers, given the significant spending differences.

Geographic Focus

Allocate more marketing resources to high-performing cities while developing strategies to improve performance in underperforming regions.
